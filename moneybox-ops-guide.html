Fund Structure Overview

Cache RE
Responsible Entity
Holds legal title to all Fund assets on behalf of investors. The central authority — all money movement must be approved by Cache RE before the custodian acts. Issues instructions to Sandhurst after reviewing and approving PIs from the IM.
Butn / Moneybox IM
Investment Manager
Manages the Fund's assets once they reach the Moneybox SPV. Responsible for loan origination, credit assessment, and portfolio management. Must submit a Proper Instruction to Cache RE for every deployment of Fund capital.
FundOps
Administrator
Handles unitisation, NAV calculation, unit registry, and distribution processing. Receives data from the IM, prepares financials, and sends distribution packs to Cache RE for approval. All submissions go via the FundOps Help Center.
Sandhurst
Custodian
Holds the Fund's money in the Operating Account. Releases funds only on instruction from Cache RE (after RE approves a valid PI). The IM cannot instruct Sandhurst directly — all custodian instructions flow from Cache.

Step-by-step — Unitisation
1
Investor
Investor submits application
The investor sends their application through the Moneybox platform. Application money lands in the Applications Account.
2
FundOps / Admin
FundOps unitises and transfers to Operating Account
FundOps processes the application, issues units to the investor, and transfers the money into the Operating Account (the custodian account held with Sandhurst).
3
Butn / Moneybox IM — Your action
You submit a Proper Instruction to Cache RE
To move money from the Operating Account to the Moneybox SPV, you must send a Proper Instruction (PI) to Cache RE via a Trustee Help Center ticket.
The PI must include:
  • 4 signature blocks — 2 from authorised signatories of Moneybox IM + 2 from authorised signatories of the RE (Cache will countersign)
  • Loan details — full details of the loan you intend to enter into with the Fund's money, as per section 5 of the Loan Agreement
  • The Product Pack report (see Reporting — Governance) must be attached to the PI
4
Cache RE
Cache RE reviews and approves (or rejects) the PI
Cache RE will review the PI, the loan details, and the supporting Product Pack. If satisfied, Cache RE will countersign and forward the PI to Sandhurst for execution. If rejected, you will be notified and must resubmit.
5
Sandhurst — Custodian
Sandhurst releases funds to Moneybox SPV
The custodian releases only the requested amount from the Operating Account to the Moneybox SPV. From the moment funds reach the SPV, Moneybox IM controls the money within the terms of the Loan Agreement.

Step-by-step — Quarterly Distribution
1
Butn / Moneybox IM — Your action
After last business day of quarter — two simultaneous actions
  • Action 1 (Data): Provide the final month's data for NAV calculation through a ticket in the FundOps Help Center.
  • Action 2 (Cash): Send the full interest amounts ($) from the Moneybox SPV back to the Operating Account (Sandhurst/Custody). This cash is what will be distributed to investors.
2
FundOps / Admin
Admin provides preliminary financials to IM
FundOps prepares and provides the preliminary financials to the Investment Manager by the 5th business day after quarter's end.
3
Butn / Moneybox IM — Your action
You provide distribution calculations and allocations to Admin
After reviewing the preliminary financials, you must provide Admin with:
  • Distribution amounts for each investor
  • Whether each investor's distribution is a cash distribution or a reinvestment (DRP — Distribution Reinvestment Plan)
4
FundOps / Admin
Admin sends Distribution Pack to Cache RE for approval
FundOps compiles the Distribution Pack and sends it to Cache RE for review and approval.
5
Cache RE
Cache RE reviews and approves the Distribution Pack
Cache RE reviews the distribution calculations, approves (if applicable), and instructs Admin to proceed.
6
FundOps / Admin
Admin distributes to investors
Admin sends each investor either: (a) a cash distribution to their nominated bank account, or (b) a reinvestment of units (DRP). Redemptions are also processed at this stage.
Fund flow
Performance Fee
How performance fees are calculated, approved, and released from the Fund. A Proper Instruction with specific invoice requirements is needed — Cache RE must approve before any money moves.
📋
Admin calculates first. You do not initiate the performance fee process — FundOps/Admin calculates it and sends you the calculations for review first.
Step-by-step — Performance Fee
1
FundOps / Admin
Admin calculates the performance fee
FundOps calculates the performance fee based on the fund's performance metrics as agreed in the fund documents.
2
FundOps / Admin
Admin sends calculations to IM for approval
FundOps sends the performance fee calculations to Moneybox IM for review and approval before any instruction is issued.
3
Butn / Moneybox IM — Your action
You approve and send invoice + PI to Cache RE
If you agree with the calculations, send both an invoice AND a Proper Instruction to Cache RE via a Trustee Help Center ticket.
Invoice must contain:
  • Amount of performance fee
  • Description of the item
  • Date
  • Bank details of the account to receive the fee
  • Addressed to the Fund (not to Cache or FundOps)
Proper Instruction must:
  • Instruct the Custodian to release money from the Ops account to the designated IM account
  • Be signed by 2 authorised signatories from Cache RE + 2 authorised signatories from Moneybox IM
4
Cache RE
Cache RE reviews calculations, invoice and PI
Cache RE independently reviews whether the performance fee is valid per the fund documents, that the invoice is correctly addressed and complete, and that the PI is properly signed. RE may reject if any element is missing or incorrect.
5
Sandhurst — Custodian
Custodian releases performance fee to IM's account
If Cache RE approves, it sends the PI to Sandhurst. Sandhurst releases the performance fee from the Operating Account to the IM's nominated account as specified in the PI.
Key requirement
Proper Instructions (PI)
A Proper Instruction is the formal mechanism for requesting Cache RE to authorise money movement out of the Fund. All PIs must meet specific requirements — incomplete PIs will be rejected.
🚫
No money moves without a valid PI approved by Cache RE. The custodian (Sandhurst) will only release funds upon receiving instructions from the RE — not directly from you.
When do you need a PI?
Required for
Deploying funds to Moneybox SPV
Every time money needs to be deployed from the Operating Account to the Moneybox SPV to finance a loan. Must be accompanied by the Product Pack report and full loan details.
Required for
Performance Fee payment
When claiming an approved performance fee. Must be accompanied by the invoice addressed to the Fund and include full bank details of the receiving account.
PI Requirements — Always
Proper Instruction — Mandatory Elements
  • 4 signature blocks — 2 from authorised signatories of Moneybox IM Pty Ltd + 2 from authorised signatories of Cache RE (RE countersigns on approval)
  • Clear instruction to the Custodian specifying the exact amount to be released and the destination account
  • Loan details (for SPV deployment) — as per section 5 of the Loan Agreement, including purpose, amount, term, rate, and borrower
  • Submitted via Trustee Help Center ticket — not by email or any other channel
PI for Loan Deployment — Additional Requirement
📎
Attach the Product Pack report with every deployment PI. The Product Pack must show the nature of the loan, nature of security, asset type, diversification proportion, and any concentration implications. See Reporting — Governance for the full Product Pack requirements.
How to Submit
Process
Submit via Trustee Help Center
  • Log in to the Trustee Help Center (contact Cache for access if you don't have it)
  • Open a new ticket — select the relevant category (Loan Deployment / Performance Fee)
  • Attach the signed PI document and all supporting materials (Product Pack, invoice as applicable)
  • Cache RE will review and respond within the agreed SLA. If approved, Cache RE countersigns and forwards to Sandhurst
